# Registration Procedures 2025/2026

## Overview
All students must register for each semester at the University of Dar es Salaam through the Student Portal (portal.udsm.ac.tz). Registration is only complete after fee payment and course selection. Unregistered students may not attend classes or sit for examinations.

## New Student Registration (First-Time)
1. Check your admission status on the UDSM Admissions Portal: https://udsm.admission.ac.tz
2. Accept your admission offer online within **2 weeks** of receiving it.
3. Pay the acceptance fee of **Tsh 50,000** to NMB Bank using your Control Number.
4. Upload the following required documents on the portal:
   - Birth certificate (original or certified copy)
   - Academic certificates (CSEE, ACSEE, diploma, or equivalent)
   - Two recent passport-sized photographs
   - Medical clearance report from a registered hospital
   - National ID or passport copy
5. Receive your student registration number (format: YYYY-CC-NNNNN, e.g., 2025-04-00123).
6. Pay tuition and other fees — minimum 60% of total fees.
7. Register for your courses online via the Student Portal.
8. Print your registration slip and keep it for reference.
9. Attend orientation week (September 1–5 for Semester 1 intake).

## Continuing Student Registration
1. Log in to the Student Portal at https://portal.udsm.ac.tz using your registration number and password.
2. Check and clear any **outstanding fees or library fines** from the previous semester.
3. View your **Fee Statement** and generate a Control Number for payment.
4. Pay the current semester fees — minimum **60%** before registration.
5. Click on **Course Registration** in the portal.
6. Consult your Academic Advisor's recommended course list for your year.
7. Select your courses (minimum 4, maximum 7 per semester).
8. Review your selection and click **Submit Registration**.
9. Print your registration slip as proof.
10. Attend classes.

## Course Selection Guidelines
- Each course carries 3 credit units (some specialised courses carry 6).
- Minimum load: **4 courses = 12 credit units** per semester.
- Maximum load: **7 courses = 21 credit units** per semester.
- You must consult your Academic Advisor before finalising your selection.
- Complete all **prerequisites** before registering for advanced courses.
- Some courses have **limited capacity** — register early to avoid missing out.
- Core/compulsory courses in your curriculum must be taken in the specified year.

## Add / Drop Period
- Students may **add** (enrol in additional) courses or **drop** (withdraw from) courses during the **first 2 weeks** of lectures.
- Add/Drop deadline: **Friday of Week 2** (September 19 for Semester 1; February 20 for Semester 2).
- After the deadline, students must petition the Dean of School with valid reasons.
- Dropping a course after Week 6 results in a **grade of W** (Withdrawn) on your transcript.

## Late Registration
- Late registration (after the Week 2 deadline) incurs a **penalty fee of Tsh 50,000**.
- Late registration is accepted up to **Week 6** only.
- After Week 6, no new registrations are accepted for the current semester.
- Late registration requires written approval from the Dean of School.

## Registration Statuses
| Status | Meaning |
|---|---|
| **Registered** | Full registration completed — eligible to attend classes and examinations |
| **Provisional** | Fees partially paid (less than 60%) — restricted access to some services |
| **Pending** | Documents under review — not yet fully enrolled |
| **Incomplete** | Missing required documents or payments |
| **Withdrawn** | Student has officially withdrawn from the semester |

## Transfer Between Programmes
- Submit a Transfer Application Form to the Academic Registrar's Office.
- Minimum CGPA of **2.5** required.
- Transfer is subject to availability of places in the target programme.
- Credits already earned may be considered for exemption by the receiving School Board.
- Transfer decisions are communicated within 4 weeks of application.

## Interruption of Studies (Deferment)
- A student may interrupt studies for a maximum of **2 consecutive semesters** (one academic year).
- Apply to the Dean of School before the end of Week 4 of the semester.
- Approved reasons: serious illness (with medical certificate), family emergency, financial hardship, pregnancy.
- Students on HESLB loans must notify HESLB of any interruption.
- After 2 semesters of interruption, a student must apply for **readmission**.

## Transfer from Other Universities (Credit Transfer)
- Apply to the Academic Registrar with certified transcripts and course outlines from the previous institution.
- The relevant School Board evaluates which courses qualify for exemption.
- A minimum of 50% of total credit units must be completed at UDSM.
- Maximum credits transferable: 60 units (for a 120-unit programme).

## Common Questions About Registration

**Q: When does course registration open?**
A: Semester 1 registration opens September 1. Semester 2 registration opens February 2. Registration closes at the end of Week 2 without a penalty fee.

**Q: What happens if I miss the registration deadline?**
A: You can still register up to Week 6 with a Tsh 50,000 late registration penalty. After Week 6, no registration is accepted for that semester.

**Q: How many courses can I register for?**
A: Minimum 4 courses (12 credits) and maximum 7 courses (21 credits) per semester. Consult your Academic Advisor.

**Q: I want to defer my studies. What should I do?**
A: Submit an interruption application to the Dean of your School before end of Week 4. You may interrupt for up to 2 consecutive semesters with an approved reason.

**Q: How do I check my registration status?**
A: Log in to the Student Portal at portal.udsm.ac.tz. Your registration status is shown on the dashboard homepage.
