# Academic Regulations and Grading System 2025/2026

## Overview
Academic regulations at the University of Dar es Salaam govern student progression, grading, academic standing, and programme completion requirements. These rules apply to all undergraduate and postgraduate students and are ratified by the University Senate.

## Calculating GPA and CGPA

**GPA (Grade Point Average)** is calculated per semester:
> GPA = Σ (Grade Points × Course Credit Units) ÷ Σ Credit Units

**Example**: If you take 5 courses (3 credit units each = 15 units total) and earn: A, B+, B, C+, C:
> GPA = (5.0×3 + 4.0×3 + 3.5×3 + 3.0×3 + 2.5×3) ÷ 15 = (15+12+10.5+9+7.5) ÷ 15 = 54 ÷ 15 = **3.6**

**CGPA (Cumulative Grade Point Average)** = Total weighted grade points across all semesters ÷ total credit units attempted. Visible on the Student Portal after each semester's results are ratified.

## Academic Standing

| CGPA | Standing | Consequence |
|---|---|---|
| 3.5 – 5.0 | Excellent / Dean's List | No action required |
| 2.0 – 3.49 | Satisfactory / Good Standing | No action required |
| 1.5 – 1.99 | Academic Probation | Must retake failed courses; GPA must improve next semester |
| Below 1.5 | Academic Discontinuation | Cannot continue for one academic year; must apply for readmission |

- A student placed on **Academic Probation for two consecutive semesters** will be **discontinued**.
- A **discontinued student** may apply for **readmission** after one academic year.
- Readmission is not guaranteed — subject to performance review by the School Board.

| Grade | Percentage | Grade Points |

- Minimum CGPA for Master's graduation: **3.0**
- Minimum CGPA for continuing in a PhD: **3.5**
- Students failing two or more courses in a semester may be discontinued from their postgraduate programme.

## Course Repetition Rules
- A student who fails a course (grade F) **may repeat** the course in the next semester it is offered.
- A student may repeat a course at most **twice**.
- When a course is repeated, the **new grade replaces** the failed grade in the CGPA calculation.
- However, both grades remain visible on the academic transcript.
- Students with a grade of E (35–39%) may attempt supplementary examinations instead of repeating.

## Supplementary Examinations (Re-sits)
- Available for grades of **E or F** when the semester GPA is at least **1.5**.
- Must apply via the Student Portal within **7 days** of results publication.
- Fee: **Tsh 50,000 per course**.
- Maximum grade achievable in a supplementary: **C (45%)**.
- If a supplementary is passed, the grade C replaces the original E or F.

## Programme Completion Requirements (Undergraduate)
- Minimum credit units: 120 (3-year programme) or 160 (4-year programme).
- Minimum CGPA: **2.0** at graduation.
- All core / compulsory courses must be completed with at least a **D** grade.
- Industrial attachment / field work completed and report submitted.
- Final year project / dissertation submitted and approved.
- All fees and library fines cleared.
- No pending disciplinary cases.

## Plagiarism Policy
- All submitted work must be the student's own original work.
- All sources must be cited properly using APA, MLA, Harvard, or the discipline-specific style.
- UDSM uses the **Turnitin** plagiarism detection system — assignments may be checked by lecturers.
- Plagiarism penalty: Grade 0 for the assignment on first offence; Grade F for the course and a formal warning on second offence; Expulsion on third offence.

## Common Questions About Academic Regulations

**Q: What grade do I need to pass a course?**
A: You need a minimum of **D (40%)** to pass a course. Grades below D (E or F) are failing grades. A grade of E (35–39%) allows you to sit supplementary exams.

**Q: What is the minimum CGPA to graduate?**
A: For undergraduate programmes, the minimum CGPA is **2.0**. For Master's degrees, it is **3.0**.

**Q: My CGPA dropped below 2.0. What happens?**
A: If your CGPA is between 1.5–1.99, you are placed on Academic Probation and must improve your CGPA the next semester. If it falls below 1.5, you are discontinued for one year.

**Q: How is my degree class determined?**
A: Your degree class is based on your final CGPA: First Class (4.5–5.0), Upper Second (3.5–4.4), Lower Second (2.5–3.4), Third Class (2.0–2.4).

**Q: Can I retake a course to improve my grade?**
A: You can retake a course you failed (F grade) — the new grade replaces the old one in your CGPA calculation. You may retake a course at most twice.
