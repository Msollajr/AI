# Examination Rules and Procedures 2025/2026

## Overview
All final examinations at the University of Dar es Salaam are governed by the Senate Examination Regulations. Students must familiarise themselves with these rules before any examination. Ignorance of the rules is not an acceptable defence for misconduct.

## Examination Eligibility Requirements
To be eligible to sit for final examinations, a student must:
- Have a minimum **80% lecture attendance** record for each course (verified by the course lecturer).
- Have completed and submitted **all continuous assessment** components (assignments, CATs, lab reports).
- Have **no outstanding fees** — students with unpaid balances are barred from the examination hall.
- Possess a **valid student ID card** (must be placed on the desk during the exam).
- Be **fully registered** for the semester (Provisional status may restrict access).

## Examination Schedule
| Type | Timing |
|---|---|
| Semester 1 main examinations | December 15 – December 24 |
| Semester 2 main examinations | June 1 – June 14 |
| Supplementary examinations | July 7 – July 18 |
| Special examinations (medical/emergency) | Arranged by Academic Registrar |
| Retake examinations | Next semester offering of the course |

Individual timetables are published on the Student Portal (portal.udsm.ac.tz) 3 weeks before examinations begin. Students are responsible for checking their personal timetable.

## Examination Conduct Rules
1. Arrive at the examination venue **at least 15 minutes** before the scheduled start time.
2. Place your **valid student ID card** on the desk — the invigilator will verify identity.
3. **Strictly prohibited** in the examination hall:
   - Mobile phones, smartwatches, earphones, or any electronic devices (must be switched off and left outside or in your bag — not on the desk)
   - Unauthorised written materials, textbooks, or notes
   - Conversation or communication with other students in any form
   - Sharing of materials (rulers, calculators, etc.) without invigilator permission
4. Students arriving **more than 30 minutes late** will not be admitted.
5. Students may not leave the examination hall during the **first 30 minutes** or the **last 15 minutes**.
6. Follow all invigilator instructions at all times without argument.
7. Stop writing **immediately** when the invigilator announces time is up.

## Assessment Weighting
- **Continuous Assessment (CA)**: 40% of the total course mark
  - Includes: assignments, CATs (Continuous Assessment Tests), lab practicals, presentations
- **Final Examination**: 60% of the total course mark
- Both components must be completed — a student who skips all CA will still receive zero for that 40%.

## Academic Misconduct (Cheating)
| Offence | Penalty |
|---|---|
| **First offence** (possession of notes, copying, phone found) | Grade F for the course + written warning on academic record |
| **Second offence** (repeat misconduct) | Grade F for all semester courses + suspension for 1 semester |
| **Third offence** | Expulsion from the university |
| Examples of misconduct | Plagiarism, copying from another student, unauthorized materials, impersonation, purchasing essays or assignments online |

All academic misconduct cases are heard by the Senate Disciplinary Committee. Students have the right to a fair hearing.

## Missed Examinations
- **Illness**: Submit a valid medical certificate from a registered hospital to the Academic Registrar's Office **within 7 days** of the missed exam.
- **Bereavement**: Submit a death certificate and a letter from your parent/guardian within 7 days.
- **Other emergencies**: Submit a formal written request with supporting documents.
- If approved, a **special examination** will be arranged at a date set by the Academic Registrar.
- **Unexcused absence** from any examination: Automatic grade of **F** for that course.

## Supplementary Examinations
- Supplementary exams are available for students who obtain a grade of **F** but have a semester GPA of at least **1.5**.
- To apply: submit a supplementary exam application via the Student Portal within **7 days** of official result publication.
- Pay the supplementary exam fee: **Tsh 50,000** per course.
- Supplementary exams are held in July (July 7–18).
- Maximum grade achievable in a supplementary exam: **C** (45%).
- A student may only sit supplementary exams for a maximum of **3 courses** per semester.

## Examination Results
- Results are published on the **Student Portal** within **4 weeks** of the last examination.
- An official notification is sent to your student email (registrationnumber@student.udsm.ac.tz).
- Results are provisional until ratified by the Senate.
- Semester GPA and CGPA are automatically updated on the portal after ratification.

## Grade Review (Remarking) Procedure
1. Check your results on the portal.
2. If you believe there is an error, submit a **Grade Review Request** via the portal or at the Academic Registrar's Office.
3. Deadline: **Within 14 days** of official result publication.
4. Pay the grade review fee: **Tsh 30,000** per course.
5. An independent examiner remarkes the script and a revised result is communicated within 4 weeks.
6. If still dissatisfied after remarking, submit a formal appeal to the **Dean of School** within 7 days.

## Common Questions About Examinations

**Q: What should I bring to an exam?**
A: Your valid student ID card, approved stationery (pens, pencils, ruler), and if permitted by the course, a non-programmable calculator. No phones or electronic devices.

**Q: I was sick on exam day. What do I do?**
A: Go to a registered hospital, get a medical certificate, and submit it to the Academic Registrar's Office within 7 days. Contact reg@udsm.ac.tz immediately.

**Q: I failed a course. Can I re-sit?**
A: Yes. If you got an F and your semester GPA is at least 1.5, apply for supplementary exams via the portal within 7 days of results publication. Pay Tsh 50,000 per course. Supplementary exams are in July.

**Q: My exam result seems wrong. How do I get it checked?**
A: Submit a Grade Review Request via the portal within 14 days of results. Pay Tsh 30,000 per course. An independent examiner will recheck your script.

**Q: What happens if a phone is found on me during an exam?**
A: Even if it is switched off, possession of a phone in the exam hall is treated as academic misconduct — first offence: grade F for that course plus a written warning.
