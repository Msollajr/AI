# Hostel Accommodation Guidelines 2025/2026

## Overview
The University of Dar es Salaam provides on-campus accommodation in five residential halls. Priority is given to first-year undergraduate and international students. All hostel residents must abide by the hostel rules and regulations. Hostel applications are submitted online through the Student Portal (portal.udsm.ac.tz).

| Hall 4 | Kambarage Hall | Postgraduate students | Single rooms |

## How to Apply for Hostel Accommodation
1. Log in to the Student Portal at https://portal.udsm.ac.tz
2. Click on **Hostel Application** — applications open one month before each semester starts.
3. Pay the non-refundable application fee of **Tsh 20,000** via mobile money or bank.
4. Select your preferred hostel block and room type.
5. Upload proof of payment (bank slip or M-Pesa confirmation).
6. Check your application results on the portal — published 2 weeks before classes commence.
7. Accepted students must pay hostel fees within **7 days** of receiving acceptance, or the room is forfeited.

## Hostel Rules and Regulations

### Quiet Hours
- Weekdays (Mon–Fri): 10:00 PM – 6:00 AM
- Weekends (Sat–Sun): 12:00 AM – 8:00 AM
- Music, TV, and loud conversations must be kept at low volume at all times in common areas after 9 PM.

### Visitors
- Visitors of the **opposite gender** are not permitted in rooms after **8:00 PM**.
- All visitors must register at the hall reception desk on arrival.
- Visitors must leave by 8:00 PM on weekdays and 10:00 PM on weekends.
- Overnight guests are strictly prohibited without written permission from the Hall Warden.

### Room and Common Areas
- No cooking inside rooms — shared kitchens are available in every hall (open 6 AM – 10 PM).
- No alcohol, illegal substances, or firearms allowed in hostels.
- No pets of any kind.
- Students are responsible for keeping their rooms clean and tidy.
- Monthly room inspections are conducted by the Hall Supervisor.
- Damage to room furniture or fittings will be charged to the responsible student.

## Check-in and Check-out
- **Check-in**: From 3 days before semester start date.
- **Check-out**: Within 48 hours of the student's last examination.
- **Late check-out penalty**: Tsh 10,000 per day.
- **Lost key replacement**: Tsh 15,000.
- Students must hand in their room key at the reception before leaving.

## Catering and Meals
- The Students' Cafeteria is located on the ground floor of the Students' Centre.
- **Operating Hours**: Daily 6:30 AM – 9:00 PM.
- Meals are available for purchase (breakfast, lunch, and dinner).
- Approximate meal cost: Tsh 3,000 – 6,000 per meal depending on selection.
- Students may also use the shared kitchens in their hall to prepare their own meals.

## Maintenance and Repairs
- Report any maintenance issues (broken lights, plumbing, furniture) to the Hall Supervisor or log a ticket at the Dean of Students Office.
- Emergency maintenance (burst pipe, electrical hazard): Available 24 hours — call security at +255 22 2410500.
- Routine room inspection: Once per month by the Hall Supervisor.

## Complaints Procedure
1. First, speak directly with your Hall Supervisor or Warden.
2. If unresolved, submit a written complaint to the Dean of Students Office (Administration Building).
3. The Dean of Students will investigate and respond within 5 working days.
4. Students may escalate to the Deputy Vice Chancellor (Academic) if unsatisfied.

## Common Questions About Hostels

**Q: How do I apply for hostel accommodation?**
A: Log in to the Student Portal (portal.udsm.ac.tz), click Hostel Application, pay the Tsh 20,000 application fee, and select your preferred room. Applications open one month before each semester.

**Q: What hostel is available for first-year students?**
A: Hall 1 (Mlimani Hall) is reserved for first-year undergraduate students and has 4-person shared rooms.

**Q: Can I have guests in my room?**
A: Day visitors are welcome but must register at reception. Opposite-gender visitors must leave by 8 PM. Overnight guests are not allowed without written permission from the Hall Warden.

**Q: Is food provided in the hostels?**
A: No meals are included in hostel fees. However, a Students' Cafeteria in the Students' Centre serves breakfast, lunch, and dinner (Tsh 3,000–6,000 per meal). Shared kitchens are also available in every hall.

**Q: What do I do if something in my room is broken?**
A: Report it to your Hall Supervisor, or submit a maintenance request at the Dean of Students Office. Emergency maintenance is available 24 hours — call +255 22 2410500.
