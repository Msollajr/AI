# University of Dar es Salaam Student Handbook 2025/2026

## Student Rights
- Access to library, computer labs, and sports facilities
- Fair treatment in all academic matters
- Appeal process for grades and disciplinary decisions
- Representation through the Student Government (UDASA)
- Access to health services at the University Health Centre

## Student Responsibilities
- Attend at least 80% of lectures for each course
- Maintain academic integrity in all submissions
- Respect university property and fellow students
- Follow the dress code (decent and professional attire)
- Pay all fees by the specified deadlines

## Support Services
- **Dean of Students Office**: Handles student welfare, discipline, and accommodation
- **Guidance and Counselling Unit**: Free confidential counselling services
- **Career Development Centre**: CV writing, interview prep, job placements
- **Disability Support Services**: Special arrangements for students with disabilities
- **International Student Office**: Visa support, orientation, cultural adjustment

## Campus Facilities
- Library: 8:00 AM - 10:00 PM weekdays, 9:00 AM - 5:00 PM weekends
- Computer Labs: 7:00 AM - 9:00 PM daily
- Sports Complex: 6:00 AM - 8:00 PM daily
- University Health Centre: 8:00 AM - 6:00 PM weekdays, 24-hour emergency
- Banking: NMB and CRDB bank branches on campus

## Code of Conduct
- No smoking, alcohol, or drugs on campus
- No harassment, discrimination, or violence
- No destruction of university property
- Penalties range from warning to suspension to expulsion
