# University of Dar es Salaam Student Handbook 2025/2026

## Overview
The Student Handbook outlines the rights, responsibilities, support services, and code of conduct for all students enrolled at the University of Dar es Salaam. Every student is expected to read and adhere to these guidelines throughout their studies.

## Student Rights
Every UDSM student has the right to:
- Access to the university library, computer labs, sports facilities, and the Student Portal.
- Fair and impartial treatment in all academic matters including grading, registration, and examinations.
- Appeal any academic or disciplinary decision through the established appeals procedure.
- Representation through the Student Government (UDASA — University of Dar es Salaam Students' Association).
- Access to free health services at the University Health Centre.
- A safe and inclusive learning environment free from harassment and discrimination.
- Access to counselling and mental health support services.

## Student Responsibilities
All students are expected to:
- Attend at least **80% of lectures** for each course to be eligible for final examinations.
- Maintain academic integrity in all assignments, projects, and examinations (no plagiarism or cheating).
- Respect university property, fellow students, and members of staff.
- Comply with the university dress code (decent, professional attire; no revealing or offensive clothing).
- Pay all fees by the specified deadlines.
- Follow all university rules, regulations, and the Code of Conduct.
- Carry their valid Student ID card at all times on campus.

## University Health Centre
- **Location**: Near the Students' Centre, Main Campus, Mlimani.
- **Operating Hours**: Monday–Friday 8:00 AM – 6:00 PM; Saturday 8:00 AM – 1:00 PM; 24-hour emergency service.
- **Services offered**:
  - General outpatient consultation (free for registered students)
  - Basic laboratory tests (blood, urine)
  - Pharmacy (essential medicines available free or subsidised)
  - Maternal and reproductive health services
  - Voluntary HIV testing and counselling (VCT)
  - Dental clinic (basic dental care)
  - Referrals to Muhimbili National Hospital for specialist care
- **Emergency**: Call the University Security at +255 22 2410500 or go directly to the Health Centre 24-hour emergency entrance.
- **Health Insurance**: The Tsh 20,000 per semester medical fee covers basic health services. Students requiring specialist or hospital care may need NHIF or private insurance.

## Guidance and Counselling Unit
- **Location**: Administration Building, Ground Floor, Room G.05.
- **Services**: Free, confidential individual and group counselling for personal, academic, and psychological concerns.
- **Common issues addressed**: Stress, anxiety, relationship problems, grief, academic pressure, substance abuse, sexual health.
- **How to access**: Walk in or book an appointment via email at counselling@udsm.ac.tz or call +255 22 2410720.
- **Sessions**: Monday–Friday, 8:00 AM – 5:00 PM.
- **Confidentiality**: All sessions are strictly confidential. Information shared will not be disclosed without the student's written consent except where there is risk to life.

## Career Development Centre (CDC)
- **Location**: Students' Centre, First Floor.
- **Services**:

  - Interview skills training and mock interviews
  - Job placement and internship opportunities portal
  - Entrepreneurship mentorship and startup support
  - Connections to industry partners and alumni

## Disability Support Services
- **Location**: Dean of Students Office, Administration Building.
- Students with physical, visual, hearing, or learning disabilities should register with Disability Support Services at the start of their programme.
- **Support available**: Special examination arrangements (extra time, large print, readers), accessible facilities, and academic accommodations.
- **Contact**: disability@udsm.ac.tz

## Student Government (UDASA)
- UDASA (University of Dar es Salaam Students' Association) is the official student government body.

- **Role**: Represents student interests to university management, organises events, addresses student welfare issues.
- **Elections**: Held annually at the start of Semester 1.

## Code of Conduct
All students must abide by the following:
- **No smoking** on campus (including e-cigarettes and shisha).
- **No alcohol** consumption or possession on campus.
- **No drugs** — possession or use of illegal substances leads to immediate expulsion.
- **No harassment** of any kind — sexual, racial, religious, or otherwise.
- **No violence** or fighting — results in suspension or expulsion.
- **No destruction** of university property.
- **No political party activities** on campus without authorisation from the Vice Chancellor.
- Disciplinary hearings are managed by the Dean of Students Office.
- Penalties range from a written warning → suspension → expulsion depending on severity.

## Common Questions

**Q: Where do I go if I feel unwell on campus?**
A: Go to the University Health Centre near the Students' Centre. It is open 8 AM–6 PM on weekdays and has 24-hour emergency service. Consultation is free for registered students.

**Q: I am struggling mentally / emotionally. Who can I speak to?**
A: The Guidance and Counselling Unit (Administration Building, Room G.05) provides free, confidential counselling. You can walk in or email counselling@udsm.ac.tz. Everything is kept confidential.

**Q: How do I find a job or internship?**
A: Visit the Career Development Centre on the first floor of the Students' Centre. They maintain a job board, run workshops, and host two career fairs per year where companies recruit on campus.

**Q: What is UDASA and how do I get involved?**
A: UDASA is the student government. Visit their office in the Students' Centre Room 101 or email udasa@udsm.ac.tz. Elections for leadership positions are held each year at the start of Semester 1.

**Q: I have a disability. What support is available?**
A: Register with the Disability Support Services unit at the Dean of Students Office. They arrange special examination conditions, accessible facilities, and academic accommodations. Email disability@udsm.ac.tz.
