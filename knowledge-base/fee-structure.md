# University of Dar es Salaam Fee Structure 2025/2026

## Overview
All students are required to pay tuition fees, examination fees, and other mandatory levies each semester. Fees must be paid before or during course registration. Students with outstanding balances will not be allowed to register for courses, sit for examinations, or receive their results.

## Payment Schedule and Options

### Instalment Plan
- **First instalment**: Minimum 60% of total fees must be paid **before** course registration in Week 1.
- **Second instalment**: Remaining 40% must be paid by **Week 8** of the semester.
- Students who fail to pay the second instalment by Week 8 will be placed on Provisional status and blocked from accessing examination results.

### How to Pay
1. Log in to the Student Portal at https://portal.udsm.ac.tz
2. Click on **Fee Statement** to view your invoice.
3. Note your **Control Number** (unique to your student account).
4. Use any of the following payment methods with your Control Number:
   - **Bank deposit**: NMB, CRDB, or NBC branches
   - **Online bank transfer**: Using internet banking
   - **Mobile money**: M-Pesa (Vodacom), Tigo Pesa, Airtel Money — pay to UDSM Government Payment via GePG
   - **Portal card payment**: Visa/Mastercard via the student portal

## HESLB Loans (Government Student Loans)
The Higher Education Students' Loans Board (HESLB) provides loans to Tanzanian students who cannot afford tuition and living expenses.

- **Eligibility**: Tanzanian citizens enrolled in accredited programmes at UDSM.
- **Coverage**: Tuition fees, accommodation, book allowance, and living expenses (depending on category).
- **How to apply**: Apply online at www.heslb.go.tz before the published deadline (usually July–August for Semester 1 intake).
- **Required documents**: National ID, birth certificate, parent/guardian income proof, admission letter.
- **Disbursement**: Funds paid directly to UDSM for tuition; living allowance credited to student's bank account.
- **Repayment**: Begins 2 years after graduation at a rate of 15% of gross income.
- **Queries**: HESLB Office, UDSM Main Campus, or call +255 22 2121026 / email info@heslb.go.tz

## Penalties for Late Payment
- Late payment penalty: 5% of the outstanding amount per month.
- Students with outstanding balances at examination time will be blocked from the examination hall.
- Graduating students must clear all fees (tuition, library fines, hostel) before the graduation ceremony.
- Students on HESLB loans who have not confirmed their loan status will be treated as self-sponsored until confirmed.

## Refund Policy
- A student who withdraws within the first 2 weeks of semester may claim a 70% tuition refund.
- Withdrawals in Weeks 3–6: 30% refund.
- No refund after Week 6.
- Hostel fees: Non-refundable after check-in.
- Refund requests: Submit in writing to the Bursar's Office with supporting documents.

## Common Questions About Fees

**Q: How do I check how much I owe?**
A: Log in to the Student Portal at portal.udsm.ac.tz, go to Fee Statement. Your current balance and Control Number are displayed there.

**Q: Can I pay fees in instalments?**
A: Yes. Pay at least 60% before Week 1 registration and the remaining 40% by Week 8.

**Q: I paid but my portal still shows outstanding balance. What do I do?**
A: Payment confirmation can take 24–48 hours after bank processing. If it is not reflected after 48 hours, visit the Bursar's Office in the Administration Building with your payment receipt.

**Q: How do I apply for a HESLB loan?**
A: Apply online at www.heslb.go.tz. The application window is usually June–August. Bring your admission letter, national ID, and parent income proof.

**Q: What happens if I cannot pay fees on time?**
A: Contact the Dean of Students or the Bursar's Office immediately. Hardship cases may be considered for a payment deferral on a case-by-case basis.
